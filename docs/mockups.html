UI Exploration · Achievement plan + impact

Where should the plan live?

Three ways to add achievements to a plan from anywhere and watch your projected completion move. All three share the same new pieces: an Impact column in the achievement list (how much each unlock raises your average, recomputed as you plan), an add button on every row, and a downloadable plan. Switch between them — real numbers from your library.

A · Docked rail

plan always visible

A permanent panel on the right, across Achievements and Optimizer. Tightest add-and-watch loop — but it costs ~320px the dense tables would otherwise use.

B · Slide-over drawer

full-width list, plan one click away

Tables stay full-width. A “Plan” pill (top-right) shows the count; clicking slides the panel over from the right. Adding a row bumps the pill and flashes a toast. Shown here open.

⇩ What “Download plan” produces (shared by all three)

A checklist grouped by game, with the target and each achievement’s difficulty. Format is up for grabs — Markdown checklist, CSV, or JSON.

# Achievement plan — nicbat
Current average: 32%  →  Projected: 34%  (+1.6 pts, 3 moves)

## Counter-Strike 2 — start (1 achievement → instant 100%)
- [ ] A New Beginning            (39.7% global)   +0.62

## What Remains of Edith Finch — finish (8/9 → 100%)
- [ ] Clear the Table            (13.5% global)   +0.11

## SHENZHEN I/O — 1/6 → 2/6
- [ ] MEET THE DRAGON            (17.6% global)   +0.16

Impact values shown are live: they recompute as moves are added, because starting a new game changes the denominator behind the average.
