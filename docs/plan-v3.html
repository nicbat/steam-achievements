1“Plan to 100%” should tick every box

Library drill-in · Optimizer finish buttons

Now

Clicking Plan to 100% stores one compact {kind:"finish"} item. In the drill list that hides every individual add-button (!finishOn), so the achievements below never show a check — and you can’t drop just one.

After

“Plan to 100%” becomes a check-all: it adds one achievement item for every locked achievement in the game. All boxes tick. Un-tick any single one and it just leaves the plan; the button flips back from “✓ Planning 100%”.

How

  • Retire finish as a stored item. The button’s handler maps the game’s locked achievements → individual achievement items and merges them in (and clears them when all are already present).
  • “All locked achievements planned?” replaces the old plan.has(finishItem) check for the button’s ✓ state — a cheap set-membership test.
  • The same swap applies to the Optimizer’s two finish buttons (near-perfect + worth-starting), so a planned game shows each of its remaining achievements ticked there too.
  • Projection / impact / markdown lose their finish branch and get simpler — a game at 100% is just “all its achievements are in the plan.”

Files: lib/plan.ts (drop finish branch), views/Library.tsx, views/Optimizer.tsx, context/PlanContext.tsx (add a mergeMany/removeMany helper).

Trade-off: a 100-achievement game adds 100 plan items instead of 1, so the plan-pill count climbs. That’s honest (“check all” really did check all), and the Markdown export already groups by game, so the download still reads as one tidy “Game — 100%” block.

2Percentages to 5 decimal places

site-wide

Right now decimals are hardcoded ad-hoc: global rates at .toFixed(1), impact at .toFixed(3), the exact mean at .toFixed(2). Tiny impact values like +0.004 currently round to +0.00 in some spots — 5 decimals fixes exactly that.

How

  • One formatter in lib/format.ts: pct5(n) → n.toFixed(5) + "%", plus num5() for bare impact deltas. Every call site points at it.
  • Applies to: global completion rates (rarity %), exact mean & projected mean, impact deltas everywhere (Achievements column, Optimizer tags, Library header).
  • Stays integer: a game’s own completion % — that’s floored by definition (the Steam method), so “33.00000%” would be noise. The displayed account average is also a floored integer by Steam’s rule; the 5-decimal precision shows up right next to it as the exact mean.

Data caveat — worth a one-line collector tweak

The collector currently rounds global rates to 4 dp (round(rate, 4)), so the 5th decimal would always read 0. I’ll bump that to round(rate, 6) (Steam returns ~8 dp) so the 5th digit is real. Re-run of the collector regenerates steam_data.json — ~4s.

Files: lib/format.ts, views/* (swap fixed-decimals), steam_collect.py + assets/collector.py (rounding), regenerate JSON.

3A “How it’s calculated” page new

new tab · pure content

A calm explainer so the numbers stop being mysterious — and so the counter-intuitive bits are stated up front.

The method

  • Only games with ≥1 unlock count.
  • Each game’s % is floored (32.9 → 32).
  • Average those, then floor again for the badge number.
  • Worked example with your real numbers (exact mean vs displayed).

Warnings ⚠️

  • Starting a new game lowers your average — it joins the denominator at a low %. Temporary, but real.
  • Unlocking 1 of 80 may not move the floored integer at all.
  • A 100% game is worth the same as a 100% game with 5 achievements — count doesn’t matter, only the per-game %.
  • Perfect games are pure ballast that pull you up.

Files: new views/HowItWorks.tsx, add key to Nav.tsx, route in the dashboard. Reuses the live averageCompletion numbers so the worked example is your data, not a toy.

Keep: “Achievements unlocked over time” (cumulative area) and “Playtime vs completion” (scatter). Cut: the completion-distribution histogram — abstract buckets that don’t tell you what to do. Candidates to replace it:

A · Your average, reconstructed over time recommended

Replay every unlock by its timestamp and redraw the Steam average month by month. You literally see it dip each time you started a new game and climb as you finished things — it makes the whole app’s thesis (and the §3 warning) visible. On-theme, uses data we already have.

B · Rarity profile of what you own

Histogram of your unlocked achievements bucketed by global rarity (ultra-rare <1% … common >50%). A “how rare is my shelf” flex chart — replaces the confusing one with a similar shape but a personal, legible meaning.

C · Closest to 100%

Horizontal bars of the games you’re nearest to finishing (fewest achievements remaining). Directly actionable and it rhymes with the Optimizer’s “almost-perfect” list.

D · Unlock cadence heatmap

GitHub-style year×month grid of unlock counts — when you binge vs go quiet. Pretty, a little decorative; less actionable than A–C.

My pick: replace the histogram with A, and it pairs naturally under the existing cumulative chart. Happy to do A + B if you want two fresh ones. Files: views/Trends.tsx.
